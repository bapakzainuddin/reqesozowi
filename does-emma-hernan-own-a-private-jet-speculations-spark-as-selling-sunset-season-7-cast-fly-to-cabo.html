Does Emma Hernan own a private jet? Speculations spark as 'Selling Sunset' Season 7 cast fly to Cabo

LOS ANGELES, CALIFORNIA: Netflix recently dropped Season 7 of 'Selling Sunset' and the viewers can't stop talking about the show.

In one of the episodes of the newest season, Nicole Young was allegedly invited by Brett Oppenheim and then uninvited by fellow cast members Emma Hernan and Chrishell Stause to fly on a private jet to Cabo.

Nicole Young dubs Emma Hernan a 'social climber'

In a recent episode of the show, things got a bit tense between Nicole and Emma when Hernan came to know that Young had called her a 'social climber.'

Emma decided to confront Nicole regarding the same at Chelsea Lazkani's 30th birthday bash. 

The two of them got involved in a heated argument and Chrishell jumped in to defend her friend Emma Hernan, due to which Nicole felt victimized.

Emma Hernan owns a food company

Emma is the owner of a food company called Emma Leigh & Co. Her love for cooking and food comes from her family. Her grandfather, Gerry McAdams, founded Yankee Trader Seafood in 1994 and now she has her own food company. 

While having a chat with Bustle, she said, “What’s really interesting about my company that people don’t realize is people just see from the [Selling Sunset] show that it’s an empanada company, but I actually bought my own manufacturing facility."

She added, "I have a state-of-the-art facility on the East Coast, so anyone that wants to manufacture an item, I co-pack for them, so there’s a whole other aspect of what I do that people don’t even realize."

She went on to say, "I own 100% of my company, which is really rare today. We’re in the ‘Shark Tank’ era where a lot of people want to play with other people’s money because it’s a lot easier. I’ve put my entire savings and everything into my company. I feel really proud to be where I am today.”

What's Emma Hernan's take on angel investing?

In the last couple of years, Emma has invested her money in various companies and start-ups. She has played the role of angel investor for many businesses. 

During the same interview, the professional model stated that this type of investing is “a lot more risky than the stock market."

Some of the companies that the reality TV star has invested in are as follows: Nanotech, Wheels, and a CBD company called Green Gorilla.

Nanotech is a big company that's creating state-of-the-art batteries that are going to change the world, meanwhile, Wheels is a bike company. 

On the other hand, she also put her money into a CBD company (Green Gorilla) before anybody knew what CBD actually was.

She concluded, "I think getting in early on different companies and different technologies and stuff like that is the way to go with angel investing.”
