James Murray Bio (Wiki)

Who is James Murray?

James Murray is an American producer, actor, and comedian. James Murray rose to fame as a member of the comedy group The Tenderloins.

The group launched a TV show on TruTV called ‘Impractical Jokers’ in 2011.

James Murray: Age, Parents, Ethnicity,  Education

Murray was born in Staten Island, New York City, New York on May 1, 1976, as James Stephen Murray. Most of the information relating to his early life and parents is not known.

He is of American nationality. Additionally, he is of British ethnic background.

Talking about his education, Murray attended  Monsignor Farrell High School and graduated in 1994.

Furthermore, he attended Georgetown University from 1994–1998.

James Murray: Career, Profession

Initially, the group of 4 comedians that form The Tenderloins met each other at a Staten Island High School. The group won a $100,000 prize through a competition on NBC.

Eventually, the group launched a TV show on TruTV called ‘Impractical Jokers’ in 2011.

The group consists of Sal Vulcano, Mike Boccio, and Joe Gatto. Some other movies and television series that Murray has been involved in are ‘Impractical Jokers: After Party’, ‘Impractical Jokers: Inside Jokes’, ‘Impractical Jokers: One Night at the Grand’, ‘Impractical Jokers Practically Live Tour Special’, ‘Jokers Wild’, ‘Criss-Cross’, ‘My Brother the Time Traveler’, ‘Return to Return to Nuke ‘Em High Aka Vol. 2’, ‘12 Monkeys’, ‘The Tenderloins’, and ‘Damned!’ among others.

As a director, James Murray has worked on several projects including ‘The Little Slutty Mermaid’, ‘Standups in Love’, ‘Criss-Cross’, and ‘Superdude & Friend’.

In 2020 she played the role of Chief Superintendent John Houseman in the British television crime drama series McDonald & Dodds.

James Murray has been hired to play the role of Major Chic Harding in Masters of the Air, an upcoming American war drama miniseries.
