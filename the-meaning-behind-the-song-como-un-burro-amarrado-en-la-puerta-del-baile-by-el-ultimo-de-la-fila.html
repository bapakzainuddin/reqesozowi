The Meaning Behind The Song: Como un burro amarrado en la puerta del baile by El ltimo De La Fila

Introduction

As a fan of El Último De La Fila, one of their songs that has always had a significant impact on me is “Como un burro amarrado en la puerta del baile.” This powerful and melodic track, released in 1993 as part of their album “Astronomía razonable,” carries a deep meaning that resonates with many listeners. Through its heartfelt lyrics and captivating music, the song explores themes of love, self-worth, and the desire for acceptance.

The Lyrics

The lyrics of “Como un burro amarrado en la puerta del baile” convey a sense of longing and desperation. In the first verse, the protagonist is faced with an unexpected goodbye and struggles to accept the idea of never seeing their loved one again. The line, “Me tiré de cabeza y me arrastró la corriente” (I threw myself headlong and got carried away by the current), depicts a feeling of helplessness and being at the mercy of circumstances.

Throughout the song, the chorus repeats the line, “Y si sólo tengo amor, ¿qué es lo que valgo yo?” (And if I only have love, what am I worth?). This reflects a deep yearning for validation and a question of self-worth based solely on the presence of love. The lyrics also touch upon societal judgments, as seen in the verses where the protagonist is compared to a thief searching for their fortune in a deserted alley—a metaphor for feeling trapped and undervalued.

The Message

“Como un burro amarrado en la puerta del baile” carries a message of vulnerability and the human need for love and acceptance. The lyrics express the struggle of feeling like an outsider, someone who doesn’t fit in or have the same opportunities as others. The comparison to a donkey tied at the door of a dance reflects the feeling of being on the sidelines, excluded from the joy and connection that comes with love and companionship.

The song also poses the question of how much our worth is determined by external factors such as wealth, appearance, or social status. The lyrics contemplate whether love alone is enough to validate one’s existence and whether the absence of certain qualities or skills pushes individuals to the margins of society.

Personal Connection

Personally, “Como un burro amarrado en la puerta del baile” has resonated deeply with me throughout various stages of my life. It serves as a reminder that our worth should not be solely defined by societal standards or the presence of external validation. The song emphasizes the importance of self-acceptance and self-love, regardless of one’s circumstances or perceived shortcomings.

Whenever I feel misunderstood or undervalued, this song provides solace and reminds me that I am not alone in my struggles. It encourages me to embrace my vulnerabilities and seek happiness and fulfillment in my own unique way.

In conclusion, “Como un burro amarrado en la puerta del baile” by El Último De La Fila is more than just a song—it’s a poignant exploration of human emotions and the quest for self-worth and acceptance. With its thought-provoking lyrics and captivating melody, this song continues to resonate with listeners, offering comfort and reminding us of the importance of self-love in a world that may sometimes make us feel like an outsider.
